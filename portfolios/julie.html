Julie is a graphic designer, illustrator, and a lettering enthusiast based out of Portland, Oregon. As an artist, her approach to design revolves around combining both hand drawn/ hand-lettering and digital illustrations to reach innovative and appealing design solutions in visual storytelling.

Why do you do what you do?

Julie: I don’t talk about this too often but the reason why I design is because of my older brother. He is a graphic designer and I was inspired by all the crazy design work he created in high school. I tried it out for myself and grew to love it as much as he did. I was basically his shadow and actually followed him to Oregon and went to the same college he went to. He is now working for the Portland Timbers as a graphic designer and hopefully one day I too get to reach that same goal of working at a great company.

What is one thing you want to learn right now?

Julie: I really want to learn embroidery! I love tedious, hands-on tasks and something about it seems very therapeutic.

What advice would you give your former/younger self?

Julie: Hey girl, don’t be so hard on yourself. No one is perfect and it’s okay to give yourself a break every now and then. There will be times when you feel overwhelmed with things but take deep breaths and tackle it one step at a time.

Sweet or salty? Cake or pie? Coffee/tea?

Julie: Sweet, Cake, & Bubble tea!

What would your alterego do, if not design/illustration?

Julie: When I was little, I wanted to be a Massage Therapist. I believed that I had “healing powers” and would practice on my parents everyday (haha)! So if I wasn’t a designer, I think I’d be a masseuse or maybe some profession in the health field.
